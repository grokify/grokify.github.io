Passing the Scrum.org PAL 1 Assessment

Since moving into a leadership position in a Scrum environment, I have been interested in extending my Scrum.org knowledge to cover leadership topics. Scrum.org has a number of resources and certificates split into two areas:

  • Professional Agile Leadership
  • Professional Agile Leadership - Evidence-Based Management

Professional Agile Leadership (PAL I) Learning Path

To pursue this learning path, I did the following:

  1. Read all the documentation, defined as follows:
    • Understanding and Applying the Scrum Framework:
      • Empiricism, Scrum Values, Scrum Team, Events
    • Developing People and Teams:
      • Self-Managing Teams, Facilitation, Leadership Styles
    • Managing Products with Agility:
      • Forecasting & Release Planning, Product Value, Stakeholders & Customers
    • Developing and Delivering Products Professionally:
      • Emergent Software Development
    • Evolving the Agile Organization:
      • Organizational Design and Culture, Evidence-Based Management
  2. Take the Scrum.org Agile Leadership Open assessment
  3. The Free Agile Leadership Essentials I (PAL I) Practice Assessment from The Scrum Master
  4. This is another free practice assessment I found out after I got my certifiation SPS Exam from Vaule Insighs

Of note, the free assessments from Scrum.org and The Scrum Master were not like the actual assessment in my opinion.
